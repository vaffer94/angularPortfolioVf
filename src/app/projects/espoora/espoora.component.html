Espoo is a high tech hotspot where they are planning to have a city as a service model to serve its citizens. It is an innovation and adventure hub and it is the most digitized city in Europe. Most of the tourists visit Espoo to enjoy different events organized in nature, or they come for international conferences and business events.

The challenge we had to work on was the following:

We started listing various groups of people connected to our challenge, mapping how satisfied they are with the current situation and anticipating how they would feel about changes in the area.

For now Espoo haven't focalized a lot on digital communication in the tourism field. Since most of the tourists come to Espoo to join events, we decided to focus on tourists as well as event organizers. So, we performed 5 interviews to them, and we discovered several problems that could be solved with the help of digitalization.

Tim is a tourist who would like to participate in some events in Espoo. He has to go on the website visitespoo.fi, and then after having chosen an event, he has to organize the travel.

The problems are that:

  • visitespoo.fi is not a famous website, so Tim can not be aware of its existence
  • for organizing the travel, he has to keep track of several apps (like Booking, Tripadvisors, GMail, Ryanair)
After finding a place and choosing the dates, she has to:
  • contact many places to find one free on the chosen dates
  • contact various hotels and restaurants, hoping that they are free as well on the chosen dates
  • organize transportation
  • consider various constraints and tourists requirements

All the process is performed among several chatting apps and social media, and all the information of the events are shared between event organizers through excel files or google docs.

  • Cultural event organizer needs advertisement and collaboration with local companies, because its potential participants are not familiar with the event content and thus not aware if it's worth going.
  • Foreign tourist needs communication ability and ease of access, because want to make the most out of the experience, avoiding missing grat opportunities.
  • Business visitors need travel efficiency and proper communicate instructions, because they don't want to waste time because of time contrasints.
Discoverability of information for tourists is difficult.
  1. Why? Because the system is not so dynamic now.
  2. Why? Because there are only webpages, physical kiosks for tickets and service points.
  3. Why? Because the city didn't focus a lot on visitors.
  4. Why? Because Espoo is in the vicinity of Helsinki and other big cities. Visitors prefer staying in these cities after visiting Espoo for any events.
  5. Why? Becaue initially they didn't focus on tourism but now they want to expand
Visitor do not prefer to download applications for information.
  1. Why? Because they prefer to look for information from other sources.
  2. Why? Because they don't want to spend time in learning how to use a new app.
  3. Why? Because it's not worth since they are going to use it only for few days.
  4. Why? Because they prefer to relax and enjoy the travel more.
  5. Why? Because they're on holiday.
HMW make the communication more effective between event organizers and tourists, so that tourists and event organizers could find, access or create, advertise more easily the events?
HMW digitalize the info point, so that tourists could be up-to-date with all the information about events in a more dynamic way?
HMW improve the communication between event organizers and local companies, so that event organizers could cooperate in an effortless way with restaurants, hotels and transportations?

Espoora consists mainly of 2 elements:

01
Web Portal
To present and manage events.
02
Chatbot
To have a smarter and more interactive access to information on events but also on the city itself.
We didn't want to create a new App, mainly because the visitors will install it and maybe delete in just few days, at the end of the travel.

Elena wants to organize a music festival, so she creates the event on Espoora, inserting dates, places and expected number of participants.

Accommodations, restaurants and other attractions can make offers, discounts or deals for tourists.

Elena can finally publicize the event on Espoora, so now tourists can see it and join it.

Tim, on the other hand, discover through the chatbot that there is a new event in Espoo and decides to go.

So, he chooses one of the suggested accommodations, pays and gets the ticket of the event.

When Tim arrives in the city, he can eat in one of the restaurants that are sponsored by the event and have a discount.

Also, through the chatbot he can get information about the event and places to visit around.

And finally, he can join the music festival!
  • They don't focus on specific cities.
  • There are no efficient channels for communication and collaboration among event organizers and other stakeholders.
  • On the tourist side, they don't offer a companion platform that supports services throughout the entire travelling flow.

For tourists, we create the value of discoverability of events through decentralized information distribution.

For both tourists and event organizers, the process is simplified because the entire process of organizing or participating in an event is in one place.

For the local companies like hotels or restaurants, we create visibility. With our platform, the marketplace is more transparent for them.

We don't want to make the user pay more just for having an easy and integrated platform to deal with the most problematic aspects of a trip.

Since Espoora provides a unique ticketing system for events, we are planning to take a percentage out of every ticket sold and every room booked.

We believe that hotels or those who offer accommodations are willing to give up a small part of the profit for a service like ours, moreover this is what the competition already does.
